The Root Cause

The Perfect Storm of Dysfunction

After 15 years of clinical practice and seeing 5,000+ patient treatments, Dr. Marcin Dorchnal and Paul Bielak identified a pattern that almost every chronic back pain sufferer shares. Modern life — 8 to 12 hours of sitting daily — creates three simultaneous breakdowns in the back's support system.

1

Lost Core Stability & Posture

When the body doesn't receive intermittent and appropriate movement, some structures become fatigued while others become lengthened, painful and weak.

2

Flexibility Restrictions

Tight hips and weak glutes create unnatural pressure through your lumbar spine during every movement. Your body tries to compensate — and every compensation creates a new pressure point. This is why "your back going out" from something trivial is actually the final straw, not the cause.

3

Movement Imbalances

Your body has adapted to the sitting position. The muscles on one side do too much, the muscles on the other do too little. When you try something dynamic — lift, twist, reach — this imbalance creates the exact conditions for injury. This is the hidden driver of "random" flare-ups.

Why Your Spine Keeps Getting Irritated

Think of your spine like a camera on a tripod. When the tripod legs are stable and balanced, the camera holds still — but when the legs are uneven or loose, the camera starts shaking. That's your spine right now.
